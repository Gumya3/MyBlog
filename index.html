My Overall Experience so far


1. My First Week At Refactory

Hello there, my name is Asiimwe Mugumya and I am a student at Refactory. I got to know about Refactory from a friend who is a developer and a software engineer. I had expressed my interest to join the tech space to this friend and he encouraged me to apply for the Refactory Catalyst Course. I applied for the course and here we are.

My first week at Refactory was quite intimidating, to be honest although with time and courage, I managed to pull through. I met some new people. They have very cool and friendly facilitators there. I also found that even though the classes were not physical, there were established communication channels in place that helped me to get along with my fellow classmates.

During the first week, we learnt about introduction to Software Engineering which was facilitated by Samson Kapeyi. This module was theoretical for the beginning part where we learnt how the internet works. Later on, the class became practical and we were introduced to things like Git, how to create online repositories, how to write HTML, how to incorporate CSS and BOOTSTRAP in that HTML and it was quite interesting.


2. My Second Week At Refactory

By the time we got to tehe second week of our study at Refactory, the sessions were mostly practical. At this time, we were talking about Git in the Cloud, forming Git branches, pull requests and merging. We also talked about the BOOTSTRAP GRID system during this week.

Personally, this was largely my toughest week so far as I found it quite hard to keep up with the rest of the class. However, due to the fact that the classes are usually recorded, I was able to go back to said recordings, revise them and cover up for the areas where I had missed.


3. My Overall Experience So Far

Notwithstanding the fact that I am still studying at Refactory and that I still have a lot more ground to cover in my journey to become a software Engineer, I have found my experince in undertaking the Refactory Catalyst Course to be quite educative and exhilarating. The modules here are not only centered about technology related subjects as there subjects here which focus on development of good habits and general personal development. We also have coaching sessions which are great because they help us to contextualise what is taught in class. I am very grateful for what I have so far learnt at Refactory and I look forward to more learning and growth on this journey of becoming a developer.
